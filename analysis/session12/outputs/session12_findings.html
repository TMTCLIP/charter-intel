1. Correlation Matrix Findings

High Correlation Pairs (r > 0.70)

political_climate × facilities_feasibility: r = 0.80 If both dimensions carry weight in the composite simultaneously, a city's political environment would be counted twice — once directly through political_climate and once through facilities_feasibility — inflating scores for politically favorable markets and deflating them for resistant ones.

Moderate Correlation Pairs (0.50 < r ≤ 0.70)

charter_saturation × replication_feasibility: r = −0.66 Negative relationship: markets with few existing charters (high saturation score) tend to score low on replication readiness. Both dimensions partially measure market maturity from opposite angles; if both carry positive weight, they may produce partially redundant signals about the same underlying condition.

academic_need × operational_complexity: r = −0.62 Negative relationship: high-need districts (low proficiency → high academic_need score) tend to also be high-complexity districts (high FRL, high ELL → high operational_complexity → low ops score). These dimensions move in opposite scoring directions for the same cities, so they partially cancel rather than compound; however, the underlying data inputs (FRL, proficiency rates) may overlap across the two dimension scorers.

charter_saturation × political_climate: r = −0.52 Negative relationship: markets with few existing charters tend toward more neutral or resistant political climates. The combination of high charter opportunity and favorable politics is rare within the NM dataset; cities that score well on both are exceptions, not the norm.

Note on facilities_feasibility

facilities_feasibility was included in the correlation matrix for informational purposes only and is excluded from all composite calculations; its r = 0.80 with political_climate is the only high-correlation pair in the matrix, meaning that wiring it back into the composite alongside political_climate would create a direct double-counting risk that is larger than any other pair observed.

Decision flags for human review

  • Collapse or re-weight charter_saturation and replication_feasibility? r = −0.66 indicates they may be measuring market maturity from complementary but overlapping angles. Human judgment needed on whether to keep both as independent dimensions, reduce one's weight, or document the rationale for retaining both at current weights.

  • Collapse or re-weight academic_need and operational_complexity? r = −0.62. They run in opposite scoring directions for the same underlying condition (high-poverty districts). Current structure means they partially cancel rather than compound. Human judgment needed on whether this cancellation is intentional or an unintended artifact of the scoring design.

  • Verify whether political_climate × facilities_feasibility r = 0.80 is a real construct relationship or a default-value artifact. Many NM cities have default 5.0 for both dimensions (scored as used_default: true). The r = 0.80 may be driven largely by shared default values rather than underlying correlation. This should be checked on non-default cities only before the correlation is treated as evidence of construct overlap.
